# Global Warming Is True; I See It in My Husband

### 
It’s 10:45 in the morning, and I have just finished cooking. My t-shirt is all sweaty and sticky, and I want to just rush to the bathroom. Once under a cool shower, I regret that I have forgotten to turn on the fan in the bedroom because as soon as I come out of the shower, I am going to sweat profusely again.

It’s mid-May of 2026. Just 21 years back same time, I brought my three-month-old baby from Kathmandu to Pune. We had recently bought a flat which was well ventilated. Though I do not remember the exact temperature then, my baby and I would spend the whole day in the living room without a fan. At night, a cool breeze would come in, and just fans were enough.

The temperature seems to follow my son and grow steadily through the years. Now I cannot imagine a single night when I could sleep without AC since the beginning of March. I try not to, but I have given up. For the past five years, I have been using AC at night for at least six hours. My son would blare his own AC the whole night. But my husband is a different story altogether.

I like to call him the energy guy. Having worked and researched passionately in alternative energy possibilities, like generating energy from atmospheric heat and exploiting water’s anomalous expansion, he is very conscious of energy consumption. He talks about saving energy all the time. So much so that he used to refuse sleeping with AC on in mid-summer till last year.

This year, the weather got him as well. Now I know for sure that global warming is real.

Using AC does not come without guilt. The whole night I keep on switching it off, hoping that the room is cool enough, again turning it on as I get sweaty within a few minutes. It eats me knowing that the cold air inside my bedroom is throwing hot air outside, making the lives of those who cannot afford even more difficult.

Who is responsible for this? We all are. Every unit of electricity we consume, every trip we make, every industry we have erected, and every waste we have generated is making the earth warmer day by day. The fossil fuel that is burnt to generate energy to have a comfortable life on earth is the very reason making our life uncomfortable on earth.

We have cut trees to make roads and made cities out of villages. We have increased agriculture to feed the ever-growing population, in turn increasing methane and nitrous oxide. Our industries are releasing greenhouse gases. The result is that the temperature is rising everywhere.

When I was growing up in Kathmandu, we did not own a fan. There was no need. A few years down the line, there used to be talks like even Kathmandu is getting hotter, and someone in the family bought a fan.

“Oh, that hot?” we would exclaim.

Now, even Kathmandu has summer, which I could not have thought of when I was a kid.

Every day, there is a list published in India of states where we will experience heatwaves, and in some other part of the country, there will be heavy rainfall, wildfires and droughts. I wonder how long we are going to survive. Extinction feels nearer than before.

According to Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), each additional 0.1°C increases the intensity and frequency of heatwaves, rainfall extremes and droughts.

Sometimes I ponder what would have happened if AL Gore had won the US presidential election in 2000. Would the world be different today? Would we have mitigated some risks? Would we not be sweating this much? For him, it was our moral responsibility to live with a minimal carbon footprint. He wanted to focus on renewable energy, lessen dependency on fossil fuels and change our lifestyle. He argued that delaying action to tackle now would be more difficult and expensive later.

No one knows the answer for sure, but the possibility of spending more on alternative energy, setting stronger emissions regulations and focusing on increased fuel-efficiency standards cannot be denied.

The US, being the largest economy and the second biggest greenhouse gas emitter, plays a major role in either worsening or combating the problem. But global warming is not only the US’s problem. India, China and EU countries need to work on it too.

We as individuals cannot solve the problem of global warming on our own, but by being more careful with the wastage of energy, recycling and reusing wherever possible, planting more trees, not making unnecessary trips and avoiding food waste, we can still contribute in meaningful ways.

I am counting days for the monsoon to arrive, as I used to for winter vacation while in school. I wish next year, at the same time when I meet my friends, we would talk less about hot weather and hot flashes. But I know I need to get used to the warmer months because that seems like the future for us.
